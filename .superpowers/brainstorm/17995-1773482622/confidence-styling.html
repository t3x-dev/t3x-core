Confidence Styling: How should uncertain frames look?

When the LLM asks "Do you prefer X or Y?", any frames extracted are tentative — not confirmed facts. How should they appear differently?

The scenario
Chat
User: I want to plan a trip to Japan
LLM: Would you prefer Tokyo for city life or Kyoto for temples? And are you thinking spring (cherry blossoms) or autumn (foliage)?
What gets extracted
✓ destination: Japan (confidence: 1.0 — user stated)
? city: Tokyo or Kyoto (confidence: 0.3 — LLM question)
? season: spring or autumn (confidence: 0.3 — LLM question)

A: Dashed border + faded opacity

Uncertain = dashed border, 60% opacity, "?" badge. Confirmed = solid border, full opacity.

C: Dashed + confirm checkbox (Recommended)

Uncertain = dashed border + checkbox. User clicks to confirm → becomes solid with ✓. Ties directly into the "human confirm" signal from the relevance model.

Note: When user confirms a frame (checks the box), confidence goes to 1.0 and it becomes a "confirmed" signal for the relevance scoring. This connects the confidence styling directly to the ranking system we designed earlier.
